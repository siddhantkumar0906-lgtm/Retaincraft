The Problem

Most D2C brands spend everything on getting customers.
Nothing on keeping them.

After the order is placed, most brands go silent. No follow-up. No warmth. No system. The customer feels forgotten — and never comes back. That silence is costing you more than your ad spend.

📭
No post-purchase communication

The order ships. The customer waits in silence. No update, no warmth, no review request. They forget you exist.

🔇
Support that feels cold and robotic

Generic auto-replies, slow response times, no scripts for common issues. Every frustrated customer becomes a lost repeat buyer.

↩️
Returns that are painful, not painless

A hard return process doesn't save you money — it costs you the customer permanently. 62% of buyers check the return policy before purchasing.

⭐
No review generation strategy

Happy customers don't leave reviews by default. Without a system to ask, your rating stays low and new buyers don't trust you.

Cloud Kitchen · Jaipur
Fitlicious
Jaipur, Rajasthan — Fitness Cloud Kitchen
Review Strategy Google Rating Customer Feedback WhatsApp Flows
The Challenge

Fitlicious had a loyal base of fitness-conscious customers in Jaipur but was sitting at a 3.6-star Google rating despite genuinely good food quality. The problem wasn't the product — it was that unhappy customers were leaving reviews while happy ones said nothing. There was zero post-order follow-up system in place.

What We Did

We built a complete review generation system: a WhatsApp follow-up message sent 2 hours after every delivery asking for feedback, a direct Google review link in every order confirmation, and a packaging insert with a QR code. We also wrote response scripts for negative reviews that turned complaints into visible acts of care.

Sweet Shop · Tinsukia, Assam
Krishna Sweets
Tinsukia, Assam
Review Generation Rating Recovery
The Challenge

A well-known local sweet shop with decades of reputation but a weak online presence. Their Google rating had dipped to 3.8 due to a handful of unresolved complaints, and they had no system to encourage satisfied customers — who visited daily — to share their experience online.

What We Did

We set up a QR code at the billing counter linking directly to their Google review page, trained counter staff on a friendly verbal prompt, and wrote personalised responses for every existing negative review. We also created a WhatsApp broadcast strategy for festive season regulars.
